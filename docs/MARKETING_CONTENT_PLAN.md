# 10 CAMP — Content & SEO Engine Plan

**Goal:** Build an organic SEO + AI-SEO content engine on 10camp.com so the business stops relying 100% on Shopee/TikTok algorithms for discovery. Every article captures Malaysian camping search demand and funnels readers to **Shopee, TikTok Shop, WhatsApp, or the Cyberjaya walk-in store** (there is no on-site checkout).

**Brand voice:** Warm, Malay/Manglish-friendly, premium-but-approachable. Tagline "Healing in style with 10 CAMP." No emojis. Lucide icons in UI, plain text in copy.

**Brands in stock to weave in:** Naturehike, Mobi Garden, Mountainhiker, Shine Trip, Blackdog (+6 others = 11 total).

**Last updated:** 2026-06-19

---

## 1. Strategy in one paragraph

10camp.com is currently a catalog-only funnel with zero educational content, so it captures **none** of the large Malaysian "macam mana nak camping," "tempat camping Selangor," and "khemah camping terbaik" search demand. The fix is a **hub-and-spoke topic-cluster model**: 4 pillar pages owning the topics we actually sell into, each surrounded by 6-10 cluster articles that answer real buyer questions in BM + EN. Because we have no checkout, **every article ends in a product-led CTA block** ("Tengok stok di Shopee / TikTok / WhatsApp / datang kedai Cyberjaya"). We layer AI-SEO structure (40-60 word answer blocks, FAQ schema, comparison tables, `llms.txt`) so ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews cite us as the Malaysian camping-gear authority — not just the marketplaces.

## 4. AI-SEO Notes (Get Cited by ChatGPT / Perplexity / Google AI Overviews)

The goal: when a Malaysian asks an AI "khemah camping terbaik untuk pemula Malaysia" or "tempat camping Selangor," 10 CAMP is named/cited.

**Structure every article so passages are extractable:**
- **Lead each section with a 40-60 word direct answer**, then expand. E.g. open "Khemah Camping Terbaik" with a self-contained paragraph naming the top pick + price range before the long version.
- **FAQ block at the bottom of every article** (4-6 natural-language questions in BM + EN), marked up with **`FAQPage` schema**. Use real query phrasing: "Berapa harga khemah camping?", "Naturehike ok tak untuk Malaysia?", "Tempat camping mana sesuai untuk first-timer?"
- **Comparison tables** for every "X vs Y" and "best of" article — tables beat prose for AI extraction and are the highest-cited format.
- **HowTo schema** on "cara pasang khemah," "cara basuh khemah," repair guides.
- **Statistics + specifics with sources** (waterproof ratings in mm, temperature ratings in °C, RM price ranges, capacity 2P/4P/6P). Concrete numbers boost citation ~37-40%.
- **E-E-A-T:** named author = the 10 CAMP team / store owner, with a one-line bio ("kedai gear camping di Cyberjaya sejak ..."), first-hand "kami test sendiri di kedai" framing, and a visible "Dikemaskini: <date>" on every page.

**Site-level machine-readable files (add to site root):**
- **`/llms.txt`** — short overview: who 10 CAMP is (Malaysian premium camping retailer, Cyberjaya store), brands stocked (Naturehike, Mobi Garden, Mountainhiker, Shine Trip, Blackdog, +6), where to buy (Shopee/TikTok/WhatsApp/walk-in), and links to the 4 pillar pages. This is how non-Google AI engines grab context fast.
- **`/products.md`** (agent-readable catalog) — top categories + representative SKUs + price ranges + marketplace links, so AI buying agents can recommend us with real data instead of skipping us.
- **`robots.txt`:** explicitly **allow** GPTBot, ChatGPT-User, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot, anthropic-ai, Google-Extended, Bingbot. Blocking any of these = that engine cannot cite us.

**Schema to implement (via schema skill):** `Organization` + `LocalBusiness` (Cyberjaya NAP, hours, geo) for local/AI-Overview eligibility; `Article`/`BlogPosting` on every post (author + date); `FAQPage`; `HowTo`; `Product` + `AggregateRating` on catalog items.

**Query fan-out:** because Google AI fans out related queries, the cluster model is the win — covering the whole pillar (sizes, brands, spots, weather) means we're retrievable for the variants, not just the exact title.

**Third-party presence (AI cites where you appear, not just your site):** keep an accurate **Google Business Profile** (Cyberjaya), answer Malaysian camping questions authentically on **Reddit (r/malaysia, r/camping)** and Facebook camping groups, and post **YouTube/TikTok how-to** videos — these get cited by AI Overviews more than our own blog.

## 5. Publishing Cadence & Workflow (small team)

**Cadence:** **1 article per week** (4/month). Sustainable for a small retail team; reaches the first-12 backlog in ~3 months and a full pillar set (~35 articles) within ~9 months. Bump to 2/week only during monsoon run-up (Sep-Oct) to catch the Nov-Jan spike.

**Simple weekly workflow:**
1. **Mon — Outline (30 min):** pick next backlog item; jot the 40-60 word answer, H2s matching query phrasing, the 4-6 FAQ Qs, and which SKUs/marketplace links go in the CTA block.
2. **Tue-Wed — Draft:** write in BM/Manglish voice; pull real store photos + an existing TikTok demo where relevant; insert comparison table.
3. **Thu — Polish + SEO:** title ≤60 chars with keyword up front, meta description, FAQ + Article schema, internal links to pillar page + 2 sibling articles, "Dikemaskini" date, author byline.
4. **Fri — Publish + distribute** (see section 6).
5. **Monthly:** refresh the prior pillar page links; update "best of" and "tempat camping" lists; re-check the seasonal article relevance.

**Reusable template per article (lock this once):** H1 → 40-60w direct answer → body H2s → comparison table (if applicable) → "Gear sesuai / di mana nak beli" CTA block (Shopee · TikTok · WhatsApp · Kedai Cyberjaya) → FAQ block → author + updated date.
